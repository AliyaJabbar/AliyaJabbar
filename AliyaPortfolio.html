About

My path wasn't straight.
It was honest.

I tried a few directions after graduation before data analytics clicked — and when it did, I went all in.

What clicked was a simple moment. I wrote a SQL query, hit refresh, and watched messy raw data turn into something that actually meant something. That feeling of impact — that's what keeps me going.

So I committed fully. 40+ hours a week, real datasets, building everything from scratch. No mentor. No one checking my work. No deadline forcing me. Just genuine curiosity about what the data is saying underneath.

I'm not someone who wants to just build systems. I want to understand things — why customers behave this way, what this number is telling us, what decision a business should make because of it.
